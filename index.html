Hi, I’m Max Kirkby. I’m a Rhodes Scholar at Oxford, where I am studying for a PhD in Computational Neuroscience. I’m originally from Adelaide, South Australia.

My research tries to understand the neural representations of hierarchical planning and the subgoals within these plans. I work with Thomas Akam at Oxford and Tim Behrens at the Sainsbury Wellcome Centre in London. I’ll present the first findings of this work at RLDM 2025 in Dublin.

Outside of research, I co-founded Rake News, a P2P news platform using LLMs for aggregating and debiasing articles. Rake evolved to provide enterprise-grade RAG to climate and finance organisations in Australia and the US. I am now co-founding Parseon, a startup that uses mechanistic interpretability to build safer medical AI.

I was formerly a Tuckwell Scholar at the ANU, where I completed a Bachelor of Philosophy (Science) in human biology, neuroscience and mathematics. My undergraduate included research across innate immunology, neurodegeneration and cardiology.
